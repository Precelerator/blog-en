Website-Builders for Landing Pages

By Fabio Maienschein
01/10/2020
No online marketing campaign can nowadays do without a landing page. Especially for smaller companies a website is the center of their online presence, but often there is a lack of budget for a professional implementation. Fortunately, there are resources and tools that make it easy to create a landing page for your project or start-up. This blog post collects the most popular website builders, with which you can quickly and easily launch your own landing page.

Drag&Drop Website-Builder

Modern website builders do not require any code at all. They allow you to hack together your own pages by using visual drag and drop editors without any programming. Such tools often offer ready-made templates into which users can insert their own content. Well-known players in this market are Carrd, Wix, Squarespace, Wordpress and Webflow, some of which will be briefly compared below:

Carrd: Carrd is particularly suitable for one-pager, i.e. landing pages or similar, which are not too complex. Carrd does not require any programming knowledge and offers a variety of templates which can serve as a starting point. Carrd offers a free plan as well as a very affordable pro-plan.

Pros: fair pricing, Free-Plan in order to try out (indefinitely), well-crafted templates

Cons: mainly useful for one-pagers (not blogs etc.), limited adaptability of templates

Wix: Often belittled as the "McDonalds of website builders", Wix promises an easy creation process for your own website. Wix is primarily aimed at people without any programming experience and abstracts any implementation details away through its visual editor. Pages can be hacked together via Wix' drag & drop builder. As often the case with other website builders, Wix pages cannot be exported as code. As a result, owners of Wix pages have to pay a monthly subscription fee to Wix in order to edit their pages. Wix offers numerous plugins to add features to pages and provides templates and a custom logo maker.

Pros: simple editor, aimed at people without an IT background, easy entry bar

Cons: dependence on Wix, after initial selection it is difficult to switch between templates, sites not always mobile-friendly, SEO of sites below average

Weebly/ GoDaddy: comparable to Wix.

Squarespace: Squarespace is another player in the website builder market. Squarespace main selling point are its professional templates which are designed by Squarespace itself and qualitatively stand out from the competition. Squarespace focuses on minimalist but high-quality design, all templates are mobile-friendly from the start. Squarespace offers detailed guides and resources and additionally provides live chat support.

Pros: high-quality templates, ability to export code, ability to change themes

Cons: no dedicated free-plan, only try-out phase, more pricey than alternatives, limited editing capabilities of templates

Wordpress: Originally started as a blogging platform, Wordpress now operates a significant proportion of all websites worldwide. With a very user-friendly process for content creation and an extensive plugin system, Wordpress has a low entry threshold, but still countless customization options. Wordpress has a large number of ready-made free and paid themes and its popular page-builder Elementor. The only downsides of Wordpress are its speed (often sluggish depending on hosting and number of plugins) and security gaps caused by outdated or amateurishly implemented plugins.

Pros: Widespread, large community, many useful templates

Cons: can be quite slow, plugins must be updated

Webflow: Calling itself the "Design Tool of the future", Webflow offers enormously extensive configuration and design adaptations. It is a browser-based website builder with a large variety of templates. Webflow is especially suitable for people who already have some website experience, as many concepts from website programming can also be found in the visual Webflow editor. Webflow allows you to export the created page as code and also offers numerous learning resources to get started (Webflow Community, Webflow University).

Pros: Powerful editor, which also allows more complex designs, many templates

Cons: for simple landing pages overkill, correspondingly higher pricing

The choice of a suitable website builder ultimately depends on your own requirements, the following questions should be answered for yourself:
  • What budget do I have available?
  • How much experience do I have with the implementation of websites?
  • How much value do I place on ready-made templates?
  • Do I want to be able to remodel or even move my site, or do I throw it away in an emergency and start over again?
