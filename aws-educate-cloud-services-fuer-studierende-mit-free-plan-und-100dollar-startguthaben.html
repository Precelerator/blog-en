AWS Educate: Cloud-Services for Students with 100$ in free AWS credits

By Fabio Maienschein
04/10/2020

With AWS Educate, Amazon provides free cloud resources to students and faculty staff in order to learn about the AWS cloud and gain experience with AWS services. In addition to various video guides on different career paths (e.g. Machine Learning Engineer, Data Scientist, App Developer, DevOps Engineer, Cyber Security Engineer, etc.), university members also receive AWS credits that can be used for cloud services via the AWS console. For students of the Munich University of Applied Sciences, this amounts to $100 per year, which can be used as a kind of playground without having to deposit one's own credit card.

The AWS platform offers a variety of useful services related to hosting (websites, web apps, backends), authentication, cloud resources (computing power, storage space) and cloud infrastructure (databases, API gateways, etc.).

How to obtain your AWS credits

The first step is to register for AWS Educate with your university email address. This can be done in the dialog that can be found here. For students that are not enrolled at the HM, the offer may or may not be available. After choosing your role ("student"), AWS Educate asks for a number of information, e.g. name, country, date of birth, email and expected end of studies.

After the registration is completed, it may take 1-2 days for your account to be enabled as AWS checks registration and student status. After that you will receive an email which should look like this:

You can set your AWS Educate password using the link in the email. After accepting the terms of use, you will be forwarded to the AWS Console by clicking on the tab "AWS Account":

Success! You've enabled your account and you can now annual Cloud Credits. It is worth to took a look at the FAQs in the Workbench, e.g. the following hints should be considered:

  • All services are only supported in US East (N. Virginia) [us-east-1] region.
  • Remember to shut down your instances when not in use to make the best use of your credits.

Do you plan to launch your own projects on AWS and are interested in getting support and guidance? The HM maintains a close partnership with AWS through the Digital Transformation Lab (DTLab) . The DTLab can provide you with contacts and support around AWS to help you get started!

Did you know? In cooperation with AWS, the DTLab organizes challenges every semester that allow students use the Amazon platform in order to tackle a series of societal problems. Head over to the DTLab-Site in order to to learn more about it.
