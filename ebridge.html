eBridge: Digital Prototyping Tools

By Fabio Maienschein
02/03/2021

Here's all the tools I mentioned during the eBridge Digital Prototyping Session from 02.03.2021.

Overwhelmed by the amount of tools? Don't be! These are all just options and you get to pick and choose whatever suits your needs. Usually you at most ever need one tool per category, and maybe Figma or its alternatives already get everything done that you want to achieve (it comes with a great community, a helpful Youtube Channel, a free plan, free pro features for students, an extensive plugin ecosystem and a whole lot more). Have fun!

Static UI Design

Figmahttps://www.figma.com/
Sketchhttps://www.sketch.com/
Adobe Xdhttps://www.adobe.com/de/products/xd.html
Invision Studiohttps://www.invisionapp.com/studio

Tools to makestatic designs more dynamic

Framer/ Framer Xhttps://www.framer.com/
Bravo Studiohttps://www.bravostudio.app/

Both these tools offer Figma imports, so you could start designing in Figma and later make your prototype more dynamic by importing your screens into Framer or Bravo Studio.

Prototyping Tools

These tools focus on dynamic prototypes with advanced capabilities like animations etc. They also offer prebuilt components that can be drag and dropped to rapidly create visually appealing prototypes. There are so many of these that I can't list them all, but you can find a nice overview on this site: https://uxtools.co/tools/prototyping

Marvelhttps://marvelapp.com/
UXPinhttps://www.uxpin.com/
Proto.iohttps://proto.io/
Protopiehttps://www.protopie.io/
Axurehttps://www.axure.com/

No-Code App Builders

These are similiar to web site builders like Wix, Weebly, Webflow etc., only that instead of sites you can build out fully functioning apps. This can be great for simple apps that only store and display data, but since you're "programming" using the visual editor of the app builder, you can quickly run into limitations of the builder itself. It's worth to take a look into the app examples advertised by the app builders to get a better feeling of what they can (and can't do) in terms of features. I'd encourage you to think through your ideal app first so that you don't end up sacrificing your initial vision by staying within the boundaries of what these no-code app builders can offer.

Glideappshttps://www.glideapps.com/
Adalohttps://www.adalo.com/
Bubble.iohttps://bubble.io/

3D-Design

This is a bit of a niche, but depending on the product this can be a very impressive demonstration and allow for a whole new understanding of a product through a 3D representation. Traditionally something you'd approach using CAD-tools, there's new tools in the space that make 3D-design more approachable even for non-technical people.

Spline.designhttp://spline.design/
Vectaryhttps://www.vectary.com/

User Testing

While in person testing is preferable to observe users reactions and to interview them in a face-to-face environment, there are ways to test digital prototypes with remote testers:

Maze.designhttps://maze.design/figma/
Quant-UXhttps://www.quant-ux.com/

Landing Pages

It has gotten much easier to build up a digital presence over the last years. Here's some tools/ services you can take a look at if you want to bring your product/ startup page online.

Simple Landing PagesWix (general purpose)
Umso(generated startup page)
Carrd (simple one pager)
More Complete SitesWordpress + Elementor
Advanced Visual EditorWebflow
