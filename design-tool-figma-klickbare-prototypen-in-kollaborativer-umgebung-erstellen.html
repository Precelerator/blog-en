Design Tool Figma: clickable prototypes made easy

By Fabio Maienschein
29/09/2020
Traditionally, there is a gap between the design and development of applications and websites: while designers often use image editing software such as Photoshop to provide static layouts and design templates, developers usually work with code in order to create clickable and interactive prototypes.
 
To bridge this gap, a variety of advanced design tools have emerged in recent years to make the design process more interactive, collaborative and closer to the development process. Leading Players in this area are Sketch (OS X), Adobe Xd (MacOS/ Windows) and the web-based tool Figma that this blog post is gonna dive in deeper into.

What is Figma?

Figma helps teams create, test, and ship better designs from start to finish.

Figma is a relatively young company in the design market and follows the mission to rethink design and make it more accessible to both designers and developers. The low barriers of entry (free for personal use, web-based, easy to create and share prototypes) make Figma ideal for projects of all types involving the design of digital applications (apps, websites, etc.). Figma eliminates the need for traditional mockup/wireframe tools by quickly creating realistic interfaces with a visual editor that look very similar to "real" applications.

Figma: Getting Started

In order to get started with Figma, head over to  https://www.figma.com/ and create your account. Since Figma is web-based, you will be taken directly to the application, where after some setup questions a short introduction is waiting for you:

If you prefer to work with the desktop app, you can find it in the web app via the menu -> "Download desktop app". For inspiration, it is worth taking a look at the example projects found in the "Recent" tab of the desktop app in the menu ("Prototyping in Figma", "Figma Basics", "Wireframing in Figma").

Features

Even in the Free Plan, Figma allows you to invite project members to join projects and collaborate with them. A key feature of Figma is the ability to work together on a design project, regardless of the operating system. This allows all stakeholders to edit and comment on the project in order to develop a design together as a team.
 
Created designs can be made available to others as a clickable prototype via the "Share" feature and can be tried out via a shareable link or even mobile via the "Figma Mirror" app.

Tips & Tricks

Design tools often have a certain learning curve due to their many different areas of application, but in Figma, this is limited to the minimum. The following key combinations and tips for using Figma are helpful for getting started:

Scroll (mouse)Scroll up/down
Ctrl+ Scrollen (Maus)Zoom in/out (zooms towards where the mouse hovers)
Space+ Drag (mouse)move view
Shift + Click (mouse)highlight several elements at once
Shift + Drag (mouse)Resize element without changing the side ratios
Alt + Drag (mouse)duplicate element
Ctrl + GGroup elements so they can be dragged&dropped together

For a quick start, we also recommend a look at the Figma Community-Section, where Figma users can obtain ready-made wireframes, templates, picture sets and icons. Especially for Figma beginners, you can find some quickstart templates here, which can also be used as inspiration for your own projects.

Resume

Figma impresses with a generous free plan, collaboration possibilities for teams, demonstrability through divisible links and a very active community that provides sample resources and tutorials on a large scale. With Figma, you can quickly develop clickable prototypes that look surprisingly close to finished applications, making it the tool of choice for all those that want a presentable demo of their idea without writing a line of code.

It's easy to get started with, and with it's vibrant community there are tons of guides and resources out there that can serve as a quick starting point for your and your application.
